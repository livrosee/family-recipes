Egg in a Hole

A delicious egg cooked over-easy inside the hole of a piece of toast!

Sick of the typical scrambled or over-easy eggs? The eggs-in-toast is a wonderful eggs-based breakfast to try! It combines both great breakfast foods—eggs and toast—into one tasty, quick-to-make (and eat!) meal. Just grab your eggs from the fridge, your sliced bread from the cupboard, and your spices from the spice rack, and you're practically set! Simply follow this easy recipe and get ready to eat a delicious breakfast.

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 tablespoon of unsalted butter
  • 1 slice of bread, any kind
  • table salt
  • ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Using a biscuit cutter, cut a circular hole into the center of a slice of bread.
  2. Heat a skillet or frying pan at low heat. Place the 1/2 tablespoon of unsalted butter onto the pan and let melt.
  3. Once butter has melted, place the slice of bread into the pan and crack the egg directly into the hole.
  4. Sprinkle salt and pepper onto the egg.
  5. After a minute or two, the egg white should no longer be transparent. Once the egg reaches this point, flip the bead and egg over with a spatula. The egg should remain stuck to the edges of the bead with careful handling.
  6. Sprinkle salt and pepper onto the egg. After 30 to 40 seconds, turn off the flame and serve!
